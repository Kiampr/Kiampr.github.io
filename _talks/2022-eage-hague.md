Presented the sequential training strategy for physics-informed neural networks (PINNs) applied to CO₂ storage simulation. Showed that curriculum-based expansion of the training domain significantly improves convergence stability for transport problems with sharp saturation fronts. Compared PINN predictions against finite-volume reference solutions on heterogeneous reservoir benchmarks.
